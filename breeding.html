Chapter 06 · Inheritance & Stats

Breed, train,
repeat.

The hidden numbers behind every Pokemon — Personality Values, Egg Groups, Individual and Effort Values, the Macho Brace, the Pokerus virus. Three sections, one playbook for the trainer who plans to win the Frontier.

Section I

Breeding
basics

Two compatible Pokemon, one Day Care, a long bike ride. The mechanics underneath, however, are mostly hidden — here’s the map.

The Day Care sits on Route 117, on the path from Mauville to Verdanturf. Drop two compatible Pokemon — opposite genders, sharing at least one Egg Group — with the old couple and walk in steps. The old man outside paces the path. If he’s facing the road, no egg yet. If he’s facing the fence, an egg is ready to be picked up. That’s the loop.

Underneath: the species comes from the female (or the non-Ditto if Ditto is involved). The father teaches compatible TMs and special Egg Moves to the offspring. Three IVs are randomly inherited from the parents, chosen across HP, Attack, Defense, Special Attack, Special Defense, and Speed. Everything else rolls fresh.

Where

Day Care on Route 117 — Mauville-to-Verdanturf path. Old man on the road tells you the egg state at a glance.

Ditto’s role

Ditto can breed with any non-legendary, non-Ditto Pokemon. Trade one in from FireRed/LeafGreen Trainer Tower or the Pokemon Mansion.

The Everstone trick

If the female holds an Everstone, the offspring inherits her Nature 50% of the time. This is THE primary way to lock in a specific Nature in Gen 3.

Inheritance · Species

From mother

The egg hatches into the mother’s base species — or the non-Ditto’s base species when Ditto is involved.

Inheritance · Moves

From father

The father can pass down compatible TM moves and special Egg Moves listed for the species. Build your father’s moveset deliberately.

Inheritance · Stats

Three random IVs

Three of the six IVs are inherited from the parents at random; the other three roll fresh from 0–31. Later generations inherit more — not Gen 3.

Egg Groups

Pokemon must share at least one Egg Group. Some — Salamence, Charizard — sit in Monster + Dragon, giving wide compatibility. Many starters and pseudo-legendaries are dual-group for exactly this reason.

Salamence · Monster + Dragon
Worked example

Mom × Dad → Egg

A Modest Latios (no — legendary, can’t breed). A real example: a female Marill holding Everstone bred with a male Azumarill that knows Belly Drum. The egg hatches as a Marill, with a 50% chance of inheriting mom’s Modest nature, and Belly Drum from dad.

Section II

Individual
values

The hidden 0–31 stats baked into every Pokemon at capture or hatch — six rolls that never change, a tax on luck the cartridge never shows you.

What they are

Six numbers, 0–31

IVs are six hidden values — one per stat — randomly assigned at capture or hatch and never changing. Higher IV, higher final stat. The difference is largest at level 100.

StatsHP, Atk, Def, SpA, SpD, Spe6
Range0–31 per stat31
Lv 100 spread31 IV vs 0 IV+31
MutabilityLocked at capturePERM
Checking IVs

No in-game judge

Emerald has no IV judge. Use an online IV calculator with your Pokemon’s level, Nature, EVs, and current stats to back-calculate the spread. Re-check after every level-up to narrow the range.

Hidden Power

Hidden Power’s type and base power are determined entirely by IVs — type ranges any of the seventeen, power 30–70. Used to give Grass-types a Fire move against Steels, Electric-types a Grass move against Ground-types, and so on.

Sub-section

Hidden Power, decoded.

Type comes from the parity (odd/even) of all six IVs. Power comes from how many IVs sit at IV mod 4 ≥ 2. Drop one IV by two to swap types — same power, different STAB.

Formula

Type & power, in math

type = floor( ((HP_odd + 2·Atk_odd + 4·Def_odd + 8·Spe_odd + 16·SpA_odd + 32·SpD_odd) × 15) / 63 )
power = floor( (sum_of_IVs_with_(IV mod 4)≥2 weighted) / 8 × 40 + 30 )

The 0–15 type index maps Fighting=0, Flying=1, …, Dark=15. Power lands between 30 and 70.

All odd, all ≥2 mod 4. The default “perfect” nuke — HP Dark on a 6×31 sweeper.

Gen 3 inheritance, briefly

Eggs hatch with exactly 3 random IVs copied from the parents (one parent, picked at random, contributes each inherited stat). The other 3 roll fresh 0–31. Destiny Knot does NOT exist in Emerald (Gen 6+). Plan for hundreds of eggs to get 4–5 perfect IVs.

Power Items don’t exist yet

Power Bracer, Power Belt, Power Anklet, Power Lens, Power Band, Power Weight — all Gen 4 items. They guarantee one IV inheritance from the holder. Gen 3 has Macho Brace (EVs only, doesn’t pass IVs). Emerald IV breeding is pure RNG.

Section III

Effort
values

Earned, not given. Defeat a specific Pokemon, gain specific EVs. Cap them, route them, double them with the Macho Brace and Pokerus, and you’ve built a Frontier-grade attacker.

The math

510 / 252 / 4

510 EVs total per Pokemon. 252 max in any one stat (255 wastes 3). Every 4 EVs in a stat = +1 to that stat at Lv 100.

Vitamins

+10 each, cap 100

HP Up, Protein, Iron, Calcium, Zinc, Carbos. +10 EVs each. Capped at 100 EVs per stat from vitamins — the rest must come from battle.

Reset berries

−10 each

Pomeg (HP), Kelpsy (Atk), Qualot (Def), Hondew (SpA), Grepa (SpD), Tamato (Spe). Each removes 10 EVs — useful to fix a mistrained Pokemon.

Watch out

Vitamins cap at 100 EVs per stat. After that, the bottle does nothing. Anything past 100 must come from KO’ing the right wild Pokemon.

Doublers

Macho Brace & Pokerus

The Macho Brace doubles all EV gain (and halves Speed in battle while held). Pokerus, a rare wild status virus, also doubles EV gain — and stacks with the Brace for 4×. Pokerus spreads between adjacent party members, so quarantine carriers and propagate deliberately.

Macho Brace2× EVs · halves Speed×2
Pokerus2× EVs · rare wild status×2
StackedBrace + PKRS×4
Method

The grind, distilled

Equip Macho Brace. Infect with Pokerus. Match a Repel to the target species’ level so you only roll one encounter. Then KO until the stat’s capped. EV training is grindy by design — routing it well is the only way to make peace with that.

  • Brace + PKRS = 4× gain
  • Repels gate the encounter table to a single species
  • Reset berries fix overtrains
  • Cap a stat before moving to the next route

The 252 / 252 / 4 spread

EVs convert at 4 EV : 1 stat point. 252 / 4 = 63 stat points; 253–255 round down so they’re wasted. Standard offensive spread is 252 in your offensive stat, 252 Speed, and 4 dumped in HP for one extra hit point. Bulky variants: 252 HP / 252 Def / 4 SpD — pick the defense based on the threat.

Pokerus, by the numbers

Approximately 1 in 21,845 wild encounters carries Pokerus. Active 1–4 in-game days; store the carrier in the PC to freeze the timer indefinitely. Spreads to adjacent party members each step. Trade with friends — infection passes through trades.

Editor’s shortcut

Hatch from the Day Care → trade in for Pokerus → hold Macho Brace → Repel to the target species → KO 32 times. That’s a maxed stat in roughly twenty real-world minutes. The Frontier rewards exactly this discipline.

Section IV

Checking IVs
without a calc

Emerald has no IV-judge dialogue — that NPC arrived in HeartGold/SoulSilver. The Battle Frontier’s closest substitute is one terse line: it tells you the single best IV. Everything else is back-calculated.

Frontier limitation

No IV judge in Gen 3

There is no NPC in Emerald that reads out all six IVs the way later games do. The Battle Frontier’s “judge” equivalents only call out the single highest IV stat — everything else stays hidden. To verify the rest, run the Lv 50 stat test below.

  • HeartGold/SoulSilver introduced the IV judge in Pal Park
  • Emerald Frontier doesn’t even have that — calculator only
  • Re-check after every level-up to narrow ranges
The Lv 50 test

Back-calc by stat delta

Calculate the expected stat at Lv 50 with 0 EVs using the formula. Compare to your Pokemon’s actual stat. The delta gives the IV.

stat = floor((((2×Base + IV + EV/4) × Lv) / 100) + 5) × Nature

Tools

Smogon Stat Calculator or psypoke IV calc — input level, Nature, EVs, current stats. Tighten the range with each level-up.

Section V

The Effort Ribbon.

Slateport City has the only NPC in the game that confirms a maxed-out EV total. She gives a ribbon if your Pokemon’s EVs total exactly 510.

The Effort Ribbon NPC sits in Slateport, near the trainer school in the north area. Hand her any Pokemon you’ve trained — she inspects its EVs and, if the total is exactly 510, awards the Effort Ribbon. It’s purely cosmetic. No battle effect, no stat boost, no follow-up reward. What it gives you is certainty: in a generation with no EV bar and no IV judge, this ribbon is the closest the cartridge ever comes to saying “done.”

Use it as a sanity check after grinding. Brace + Pokerus, Repel-locked encounters, the right route — and then a Slateport visit to confirm the math. Sweep the Frontier with whatever’s in your party next.

Where

Slateport City — trainer school area, ribbon-giver NPC. Inspects the lead Pokemon’s EVs.

Condition

EVs total exactly 510. Common spreads: 252 / 252 / 4, 252 / 252 / 6 (but 4 already maxes the third).

Reward

Cosmetic ribbon. No stat or battle effect. Treat it as the only “EV training complete” receipt the game prints.
