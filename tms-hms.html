Chapter 08 · Tutors & Disks

Moves of the
world — carried

Twenty-four moves you carry on cartridges, glass disks, and gym-leader handshakes. Eight HMs unlock the map; sixteen TMs handpicked from the long list reshape any teambuild. Below: where to find each, who should learn it, and which one-shots from the Mauville Game Corner are worth your coins.

Editor’s Note

In Generation 3, every TM is a single-use disk — teach it once and it’s gone forever. That changes the calculus. Bulk Up isn’t a sticker you slap on every Fighting type; it’s a decision. Earthquake can only be taught to one creature on your cartridge unless you grind another. Choose your pupils carefully — this page assumes you’re running a six-Pokemon party and want to maximize coverage without wasting disks. HMs, by contrast, are reusable forever; teach them to your designated field-utility slave and never look back.

Coin-Caged Treasure

Six of these TMs hide behind Mauville Game Corner’s coin-exchange counter: Ice Beam, Thunderbolt, Flamethrower, Shadow Ball, and Psychic alternates. Stock is unlimited but the coin grind is brutal — budget two real-time hours per 4×,000-coin disk, or buy coins outright at 1000 P / 50 coins.

The Eight

Hidden
machines

Reusable forever. Required to traverse Hoenn. Each unlocks a route segment, so designating an HM slave — usually Linoone, Tropius, or a spare Zigzagoon — saves your team slots.

HM01 · Field

Cut

normal
LocationCutter’s House, Rustboro (post-Devon Goods)
Field useSlices small trees blocking routes.
RecipientHM slave (Zigzagoon, Linoone, Tropius). Mediocre 50 BP move.

FIELD TIP — Required to enter Rusturf Tunnel side-routes and several gym entrances.

HM02 · Field

Fly

flying
LocationRoute 119, after the rival fight near the Weather Institute.
Field useFast travel between visited towns.
RecipientSwellow, Pelipper, Altaria, Crobat. 70 BP, ~95 acc Flying STAB.

FIELD TIP — Swellow with Guts + Facade is a battle-worthy Fly user.

HM03 · Field

Surf

water
LocationWally’s uncle in Petalburg, after defeating Norman.
Field useMandatory for over-water travel; opens Hoenn’s sea-map.
RecipientSwampert, Tentacruel, Walrein, Milotic. 95 BP STAB — the best HM in the game.

FIELD TIP — Surf is non-negotiable on any Water type. Even non-Water Pokemon can learn it (e.g., Linoone).

HM04 · Field

Strength

normal
LocationRusturf Tunnel, from the man you rescued earlier.
Field usePushes boulders; opens Fiery Path, Granite Cave depths.
RecipientLinoone, Hariyama, Slaking. 80 BP physical Normal.

FIELD TIP — Linoone with Strength + Rock Smash + Cut + Surf is the canonical four-HM slave.

HM05 · Field

Flash

normal
LocationGranite Cave, boy on the first floor.
Field useLights dark caves. Drops opponent accuracy in battle.
RecipientAnything you don’t mind blanking a slot on. Skip after Granite Cave.

FIELD TIP — Only one cave (Granite) actually requires it. Teach, use, then forget at the Move Deleter in Lilycove.

HM06 · Field

Rock Smash

fighting
LocationMauville, NPC house near the gym.
Field useSmashes cracked boulders; sometimes triggers wild encounters.
RecipientHariyama, Breloom, or HM-slave Linoone. 40 BP, 50% Def-drop on hit.

FIELD TIP — Smashing rocks in Granite Cave can yield Geodude or Aron encounters.

HM07 · Field

Waterfall

water
LocationEver Grande City, at the Champion’s island entrance.
Field useClimb waterfalls. Required to reach Pokemon League.
RecipientWhichever team Water type already knows Surf. 80 BP Water STAB.

FIELD TIP — Stack Surf + Waterfall on the same Pokemon to free a slot.

HM08 · Field

Dive

water
LocationSteven gives it after the Mossdeep Space Center sidequest.
Field useDescend into underwater routes; required for Sealed Chamber and Sootopolis.
RecipientSame Surf carrier. 80 BP charge-turn Water move — skip in battle, keep for travel.

FIELD TIP — Without Dive you cannot enter Sootopolis the first time, blocking the Kyogre/Groudon endgame.

The Sixteen

Technical
machines

One-shot disks. Pick recipients with intention. The list below is a curated selection of the most playthrough-relevant TMs — coverage staples, gym rewards, and the Game Corner’s priciest jewels.

TM03 · Wallace

Water Pulse

water

Wallace gives it after the 8th gym. 60 BP, 30% confusion. Great for Manectric or Water types without Surf.

Sootopolis — gym reward
TM06 · Field find

Toxic

poison

Fiery Path, requires Strength. Stall staple — Badly Poisons over time. Put on a bulky tank.

Fiery Path — pickup
TM08 · Brawly

Bulk Up

fighting

+1 Atk, +1 Def. Blaziken, Hariyama, and Breloom love it. Brawly hands it over after the 2nd gym.

Dewford — gym reward
TM13 · Game Corner

Ice Beam

ice

Mauville Game Corner (4000 coins) or Abandoned Ship. 95 BP, 10% freeze. The premier Dragon-killer.

Mauville — coin exchange
TM24 · Game Corner

Thunderbolt

electric

4000 coins or post-game New Mauville quest. 95 BP, 100 acc, 10% para. Best Electric STAB available.

Mauville — coin exchange
TM26 · Cavern

Earthquake

ground

Seafloor Cavern, mid-late game. 100 BP STAB for Ground types. Universal coverage — put it on everything.

Seafloor Cavern — pickup
TM27 · Pacifidlog

Return

normal

Power scales with friendship. Max friendship = 102 BP STAB-able Normal. Best on Linoone, Slaking.

Pacifidlog Town — gift
TM29 · Victory Road

Psychic

psychic

Victory Road or Lilycove Dept Store (3500 P). Best Psychic STAB — Gardevoir/Alakazam mainstay.

Victory Road — pickup
TM30 · Game Corner

Shadow Ball

ghost

Mauville Game Corner (1500 coins). 80 BP, 20% Sp. Def drop. Coverage for Psychic and Ghost matchups.

Mauville — coin exchange
TM35 · Game Corner

Flamethrower

fire

4000 coins or hidden in Fiery Path. Best Fire STAB. 95 BP, 100 acc, 10% burn.

Mauville — coin exchange
TM38 · Lavaridge

Fire Blast

fire

Gift in Lavaridge or Lilycove Dept Store. 120 BP, 85 acc. High-roll alternative to Flamethrower.

Lavaridge Town — gift
TM39 · Roxanne

Rock Tomb

rock

Roxanne’s gym reward. 50 BP — weak, but the Speed drop saves slow attackers in early-game routes.

Rustboro — gym reward
TM40 · Winona

Aerial Ace

flying

Winona’s gym reward. 60 BP, never misses. Standard Flying coverage for non-Flying types like Absol or Linoone.

Fortree — gym reward
TM43 · Slateport

Secret Power

normal

Slateport Market gift after the Norman fight. Required to make Secret Bases. 70 BP with terrain-based status.

Slateport — gift
TM45 · Lilycove

Attract

normal

Lilycove Dept Store and Slateport gift. Stalls opposite-gender opponents. Frontier MVP — female Pokemon dominate.

Lilycove — dept store
TM50 · Flannery

Overheat

fire

Flannery’s gym reward. 140 BP — lowers user’s Sp. Atk two stages. Hit-and-run; pair with U-turn or switch.

Lavaridge — gym reward
Field Note

The Move Deleter lives in Lilycove City. He removes any move — including HMs — so you can free up slots once a route is cleared. Pair him with the Move Tutors found across Hoenn (Frontier teaches Frenzy Plant, Hydro Cannon, Blast Burn after Hall of Fame) for full move-economy control.
