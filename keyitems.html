Chapter 13 · The Trainer’s Toolkit

Things that stay
in your bag

Bikes, rods, scopes, tickets, eight HMs, and the field cues you’re meant to read — cracked rocks, ash grass, dive shimmer, ice tiles, ledge slants. Section One

The permanent
bag pocket

Key items can’t be tossed, can’t be sold, and don’t take inventory slots from your potions or berries. Most arrive on a story beat — a few (Soot Sack, Scanner, Coin Case) are easy to walk past and never pick up.

Pokemon Emerald gives you a five-pocket bag from the start: Items, Key Items, Pokeballs, TMs/HMs, and Berries. Unlike FireRed/LeafGreen, there is no separate TM Case or Berry Pouch as a key item — sorting is automatic. What follows is every key item the cartridge contains, in roughly the order you’ll meet them. Bold entries are story-mandatory; the rest are sidequest pickups, two of which (Scanner, Magma Emblem) gate Emerald-exclusive plot beats you won’t see in Ruby or Sapphire.

No. 01 · Rydel’s Cycles, Mauville

Mach Bike / Acro Bike

After Brawly, Rydel hands you one bike and lets you swap any time, free. Mach climbs slopes, falls through cracked floors, and outruns Pacifidlog currents. Acro hop-jumps white rails and wheelies safely across Sky Pillar tiles.

No. 03 · Glass Workshop, Route 113

Soot Sack

Old man inside the Workshop hands it over after Lavaridge. From that moment forward, every step in ash grass adds 1 soot to your sack — tradeable at the Workshop counter for flutes and the unique Pretty Chair / Pretty Desk decorations.

1000 ash steps = a Blue Flute. 8000 = the Pretty Desk. Tedious but the only legitimate path to those decorations.

No. 04 · Sootopolis flower shop

Wailmer Pail

A watering can given by the NPC across from the Sootopolis Pokemon Center. Two functions: water Berry plots (halves growth time per stage if applied at every stage), and wake the sleeping Wailmer blocking Route 128 post-Kyogre.

No. 05 · Route 120, Steven cutscene

Devon Scope

Steven hands you the Devon Scope on the long bridge of Route 120 to reveal the invisible Kecleon blocking your path. From then on it reveals every wild Kecleon scattered across Hoenn — required progression, no Sudowoodo equivalent in this game.

There is no Sudowoodo in Hoenn. The Devon Scope is purely a Kecleon-and-bridge tool.

No. 06 · Jagged Pass summit (Emerald-only)

Magma Emblem

Lying on the ground at the top of Jagged Pass after the Mt. Chimney plot. Opens the hidden door at the Magma Hideout entrance — the Groudon arc in Emerald is gated behind picking this up.

In Ruby this item doesn’t exist; in Sapphire there’s no Magma plot at all. It’s the most distinctive Emerald-only key item.

Section Two

Reading the
map tiles

Every walkable surface in Hoenn carries a quiet instruction. A grey rock with a hairline crack wants Rock Smash. A spider-webbed cave floor wants the Mach Bike. A shimmering navy patch on otherwise-blue ocean wants Dive. Below: 20 tile types, what they look like, and what to do.

The one most-asked question

“What do I do with the cracked rocks in caves?” Face the rock and use HM06 Rock Smash from the menu (or assign it to a party slot). It crumbles, sometimes triggering a wild Geodude/Graveler battle. The dents and hairline cracks on cave walls are decorative; the freestanding boulders with the visible crack texture in the path are the breakable ones. First seen in Granite Cave B1 corridor.

Mach vs. Acro on cracked floors

Mach Bike at full speed across cracked floor tiles = you fall through to the floor below. Used in Magma Hideout and Mirage Tower. Acro Bike wheelie (hold B + tap a direction) over the SAME tile pattern = floor stays solid. The Sky Pillar requires the Acro wheelie path. Bring an Acro before climbing — Mach gets you stuck and forces a reset.

Cracked rocks

Rock Smash targets

Single grey rock with hairline-crack texture sitting in the path. First in Granite Cave B1, then Route 114 to Meteor Falls. Smash sometimes triggers a wild Geodude.

Cracked floor tiles

Mach drop-throughs

Spider-web crack pattern on cave floors. Mach across at full speed = fall to the floor below. Magma Hideout, Mirage Tower, Sky Pillar use these intentionally.

Sky Pillar weak floors

Acro wheelie path

Some “ordinary” tiles hide cracks. Use Acro Bike, hold B + tap direction = wheelie hops without breaking the tile. Mach will trap you. Required for Rayquaza.

Strength boulders

Push, don’t pull

Large rounded grey boulders, usually on pressure plates. Walk into one with HM04 Strength taught and it pushes one tile. Misalign and exit / re-enter to reset boulder positions.

Cut bushes

Single-tile dark tufts

Distinct dark-green tufted sprite, smaller than tall grass, blocks paths. HM01 Cut clears the tile until the map reloads. Hidden items hide behind cut trees on Routes 119, 120, 123.

Surfable water edges

Light-blue shore tiles

Face the water from a shore tile, use HM03 Surf. Water encounter rate is 4-5x higher than grass — bring repels for travel.

Dive spots

Navy shimmer patches

Dark navy in a sea of regular blue, with a subtle shimmer animation. Surf onto one, open menu, HM08 Dive. Underworld map opens; Dive again to surface.

Waterfalls

Vertical foam streaks

Vertical white foam on a rock wall. Surf to the base, face up, HM07 Waterfall. Climbs both ways — you can ride down too. First needed at Meteor Falls upper.

Sandstorm desert

Route 111 north

Tan tiles with a constant blowing-sand overlay. The route guard refuses entry without Go-Goggles. Inside: Mirage Tower (8 floors), fossil pick, Trapinch, Cacnea, Sandshrew.

Ash grass

Route 113 only

Long grass on tan/grey terrain with grey ash piles overlaid. Walk through (Soot Sack auto-equipped) for +1 soot per step. Walking circles on a 5x5 patch farms fast.

Soft Soil

Berry plots

Brown circular dirt patches scattered along most routes. Stand on one, open Berries pocket, plant. Wailmer Pail halves growth time per stage.

Ledges

One-way slants

Slanted dirt-edge tile between two terrain heights. Press toward the ledge to auto-hop down. One-way only. Trainers can’t pursue across; use them to skip line-of-sight.

Trick House puzzles

Route 110 scrolls

Each scroll-room is a logic puzzle — mazes, button hunts, cup-and-ball. Reopens after every gym badge. Eight rooms total. Rewards: Hard Stone, Smoke Ball, Magnet, TMs, Itemfinder.

Sootopolis ice puzzle

Slide and crack

Press a direction, slide until hitting a wall. Each tile cracks under foot — sliding over a cracked tile a second time drops you to the previous floor. Plan paths to step on each tile only once.

Pacifidlog currents

Animated arrow water

Surfable tiles with an animated arrow texture. Press perpendicular to break out; with the current accelerates. Mach Bike doesn’t help on water. Sky Pillar route weaves through chains — save before threading.

Mt. Pyre

Stairs & graveyard

Multi-floor mountain split into outdoor graveyard tiers and indoor floors. Summit hosts the Red Orb / Blue Orb plot. Ghost-type encounters concentrate on indoor floors. Cleanse Tag sold here.

Berry growth stages

Five visual stages

Planted → sprout → taller → bloom → fruit. Real-time hours per stage (3h Cheri, 96h+ Liechi). Water at each stage with Wailmer Pail for max yield — soil dries between stages.

Underwater seaweed trainers

Dive battles

Dark-green seaweed clumps hide line-of-sight trainers underwater. They Surf-then-Dive at you on contact. You cannot flee mid-fight underwater — finish or KO.

Fishing animation

Press A on “!”

Cast → wait → “!” bite → press A. The Oh-A-bite window is short, about one second. Higher-tier rod = faster bites and broader pool. Mistime and the fish escapes.

Things NOT in Emerald

Skip the search

Honey trees, swarms, Soaring, Rotom forms, day/night encounter shifts — none of these exist in Gen 3. Honey/swarms = Gen 4. Soaring = ORAS. Don’t waste time looking.

Section Three

Carving a
secret base

Hoenn lets you punch a hideout into nearly any cliff face, knothole, or thick yellow shrub on the map. The Hidden Secrets chapter mentions the system in passing — here is the full picture.

Step 01 · TM43

Get Secret Power

A man near the Route 111 desert entrance (south end) hands you TM43 Secret Power free after a short dialogue. This is the only way to claim or move a base.

  • Use Secret Power on any valid terrain tile to carve a base.
  • Use again on a different valid spot to relocate — all decorations transfer.
  • Valid terrain: tree knotholes, dirt-cliff indents, red flowers in tall grass, yellow shrubs.
Step 02 · Decorate

Decoration sources

  • Lilycove Department Store 5F · the bulk of the catalogue, paid in cash.
  • Lilycove Mart Decoration counter · smaller secondary inventory.
  • Slateport Energy Drink shop · a handful of niche items.
  • Trick House rewards · Hard Stone, Magnet, etc. count as decorations.
  • Battle Frontier exchange · BP for premium decorations.
  • Contest Master Rank trophies · one Trophy per contest type cleared.

Visiting other bases

Mix Records

Mix Records via wireless adapter or link cable at any Pokemon Center. The other player’s base appears in your overworld at their chosen spot. Talking to the trainer NPC inside lets you battle them — once per real-time day.

In 2026 with no native online, this is mostly emulator multi-cart save mixing or curated community packs.

Battle Frontier sync

Cosmetic only

Battle Tower’s Multi-Battle partner uses a copy of your party but does not interact with your base. Battles inside others’ bases are standard 3v3; mat-based weather effects are commonly rumored but not present in vanilla Emerald.

Placement tips
  • Use Secret Power on the tree directly outside Mauville — central, fastest fly access.
  • Set base near the Day Care for hatching trips.
  • Routes 111, 113, 114, 115, 116, 119, 120, 121, 127, 128 and a handful of caves all support bases.
  • Save before placing rare decorations — there is no “undo” for one-of-a-kind Trophies.
  • Combine slide mats with hop tiles for a tiny in-base arcade.

Registration rules

How trainers join your list

Most trainers register automatically right after defeat — they hand you their PokeNav number on the spot. A few require dialogue continuation. Always A through after route battles or registration may not stick.

  • Youngster Calvin (Route 102)
  • Lass Haley (Route 117)
  • Bug Maniac (Route 119, gives rotating Hyper Potion)
  • Hex Maniac, Kindler, Black Belt — common rematch sources
  • Ace Trainer pairs late-game (high payouts)
Rematch mechanics

Star = ready, scaling = automatic

Trainers level up randomly each time they call. The PokeNav star indicates “wants to rematch.” Approaching them on the route initiates the rematch — their party scales across 4 power-up tiers. Final tier matches Frontier opening levels (~Lv 50-60). Each badge and the post-Champion flag advance their progression.

Gym leaders cannot be rematched in vanilla Emerald — no Trainer Hill rotation, no Sevii equivalent. The Elite Four can be re-challenged endlessly.

Section Six

The cartridge
clock

Emerald has a battery-backed real-time clock. On dead-battery cartridges, every event below freezes and the boot screen warns “time cannot be checked.” A CR1616 replacement (or any emulator) restores it.

Daily

Lottery (Lilycove PC)

5-digit roll vs. your party/box Pokemon IDs. Match consecutive digits from the right: 1 → PP Up, all 5 → Master Ball. Trade-acquired Pokemon vary OT — keep a few different IDs to maximize odds.

Daily roll, free entry
Continuous

Berry growth

5 stages real-time: planted → sprout → taller → bloom → fruit. Total time 3h (Cheri/Chesto/Pecha/Rawst) up to 96h+ (Liechi, Ganlon). Wailmer Pail halves per stage if watered each time.

Hardware

RTC battery (CR1616)

Original carts run a CR1616 battery. When dead, all daily events freeze. Replace with iFixit kit / soldering iron, or sidestep with an emulator entirely.

Battery warning

If you boot your cartridge in 2026 and see “The internal battery has run dry”, the lottery, berries, tides, and Berry Master daily phrases all stop. Pokemon you catch still save fine — only RTC events break. Do not panic; replace the battery, your save survives.

Section Seven

The Glass
Workshop

Route 113’s Glass Workshop is the only sink for the soot you collect from the route’s ash grass. Trade soot for flutes (single-use battle items) and the unique Pretty Chair / Pretty Desk decorations.

Soot collection

+1 per ash-grass step

Equip the Soot Sack — auto-equipped from Key Items pocket. Walk through ash grass on Route 113 — each step adds 1 soot. Open the sack to view current count. Walking ~150 steps/min through ash is a typical farm rate.

Tip

Walking circles on a 5x5 ash patch is the fastest method. Bike on dirt skips collection — the bike-on-ash interaction is unverified, walking remains safe.

Section Eight

Move
tutors

Three flavors of tutor in Hoenn: free Move Reminder (Slateport, Heart Scale-paid), free Move Deleter (Fallarbor — the only legitimate way to forget an HM), and the Battle Frontier’s BP-paid menu of 21 utility moves. None of them are one-shots; tutor freely.

Move Reminder

House south of the Pokemon Center, the “Move Maniac.” Re-teaches any move in the target Pokemon’s level-up movepool — including ones it hasn’t reached the level for yet.

House on the left side of Fallarbor Town. Deletes any move — including HMs. The only legitimate way to remove an HM in Gen 3.

No elemental punches in Emerald

Fire Punch / Ice Punch / ThunderPunch tutors are Gen 4 only (HGSS / DPPt). In Emerald they’re only available via natural learn lists (Hitmonchan’s level-up). Use TM elemental coverage instead.

Pack the scope.
Bring both bikes.

The bag is small but the choices it represents are most of the game. The Mach Bike opens Mt. Chimney before the 4th gym; the Acro Bike unlocks Rayquaza. Read the floor before you ride. Read the rock before you smash it. Read the water before you Dive.
