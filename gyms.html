Chapter 01 · The Gym Circuit

The eight
gym leaders

Eight battles, eight badges, eight TMs. Below: full team rosters, level caps, recommended counters, and a strategic note for each leader. Anchor links jump straight to a gym.

A note on rematches

Vanilla Emerald has no gym leader rematches — the PokeNav Match Call system in Hoenn lists field trainers only, never leaders. Gym-leader rematch teams were added retroactively in Omega Ruby/Alpha Sapphire, where every leader scales up to a stronger Lv 50–60 squad with held items. If a guide tells you to call Norman for a rematch on cart, it’s ORAS, not Emerald.

Strategy. Nosepass has Harden and Rock Tomb. The Speed drop is what kills slow physical attackers, so prefer Special Attackers. A Lotad from Route 102 evolved into Lombre is excellent. Avoid leading Torchic alone unless it has Double Kick (Lv 16, train one extra level). Sturdy means a clean OHKO is impossible at full HP — chip first, then commit.

FIELD TIP — Backtrack to Petalburg Woods for a Shroomish; it evolves into Breloom with Mach Punch by Lv 23.

Strategy. Makuhita uses Bulk Up to snowball. Don’t let it set up — hit it on turn one with a strong Flying or Psychic attack. Taillow’s Wing Attack makes this gym trivial. Beware Vital Throw, which never misses.

FIELD TIP — Pick up a Whismur in Rusturf Tunnel; it evolves into Loudred and learns Stomp.

FIELD TIP — After defeating Wattson, talk to him again for the New Mauville generator quest, rewarding TM24 Thunderbolt.

FIELD TIP — Hot Springs in Lavaridge heal your team for free. The old woman gives you an Egg containing Wynaut.

Hariyama (Vital Throw), Breloom (Sky Uppercut), Combusken/Blaziken, Machoke from Granite Cave
Abilities
Slaking = Truant (loafs every other turn — this is the entire fight). Vigoroth = Vital Spirit (sleep-immune; Yawn fails). Linoone = Pickup. No held items in vanilla Emerald.
TM reward
TM42 Facade
Badge perk
Lv 70 obedience · HM03 Surf field use
Strategy. Slaking has Truant — it loafs every other turn. Use the loafing turn to Bulk Up, heal, or switch in a wall. Hariyama with Vital Throw and Belly Drum is the textbook counter. Don’t bring a Ghost type — he carries Faint Attack. Slaking’s Yawn forces switches; bring an Awakening or Insomnia/Vital Spirit.

FIELD TIP — Norman is your father in this game. The dialogue around the gym fight is some of the franchise’s best.

FIELD TIP — After Winona, head to Mt. Pyre. The team plot kicks off and you’ll need Fly to keep up.

FIELD TIP — Pick up the Sun Stone from the meteorite-loving NPCs near the Space Center after the gym — the only one available pre-Frontier.

Wallace

Ace
Milotic · Lv 43

Lv 43
Team

Roster

Key move
Recover + Ice Beam + Surf + Toxic
Counters
Manectric/Magneton (Thunderbolt), Sceptile (Leaf Blade), Breloom (Giga Drain), Roselia
Abilities
Milotic = Marvel Scale (1.5× Def when statused — do NOT paralyze; burn or sleep instead). Sealeo = Thick Fat (resists Fire AND Ice). Whiscash = Oblivious. No held items.
TM reward
TM03 Water Pulse
Badge perk
All-level obedience · HM07 Waterfall field use
Strategy. Milotic has Recover + Marvel Scale, so it’s spongy. Do NOT status it — Marvel Scale activates on any status and boosts her Def 1.5×, swinging the matchup against you. Out-damage with Thunderbolt/Leaf Blade spam, or trade Recover PP. Whiscash is doubly weak to Grass. Don’t bring the Mudkip line solo — Milotic outclasses it. In Emerald, Wallace is the gym leader (R/S had him too; remakes split him from Juan).

FIELD TIP — After Wallace’s gym you trigger the Sootopolis Kyogre/Groudon climax. Stock Ultra Balls, Max Repels, and Revives before Cave of Origin.
