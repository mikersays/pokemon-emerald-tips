Chapter 07 · Hours 0–5

The first
five hours

From the back of the moving truck to the summit of Mt. Chimney — what to catch, what to buy, what to talk to, and the easy items most trainers walk past on a first run. Five phases, in order, with the must-do split from the worth-doing.

01 Truck to Route 101 First 30 min Tutorial 02 Petalburg → Rustboro Hour 1–2 Woods 03 Dewford & Granite Hour 2–3 Cave 04 Mauville & Beyond Hour 3–4 Bike 05 Mt. Chimney Hour 4–5 Summit
Phase 01 · Truck → Route 101

First
thirty minutes.

You arrive in Littleroot in the back of a moving truck. May or Brendan hands you a Poke Ball off Birch’s belt. The first thirty minutes are setup — one starter, one Wurmple, one chat with your dad.

Choose Mudkip if it’s your first Emerald playthrough — the easiest run in the cartridge by a wide margin. Swampert solos most of the gym circuit on raw bulk and Water/Ground typing. Torchic is the second-easiest pick (Blaziken comes online at Roxanne with Double Kick at Lv 16). Treecko is the artisan run — Sceptile is fast and frail, and you’ll switch a lot.

Money tip

Petalburg Woods has a free bag-grab tutorial — the Devon researcher hands you a Great Ball, and the brief skirmish with the Aqua/Magma grunt clears free Potions onto your map.

Must do
  • Choose Mudkip on a first run — statistically the easiest
  • Catch a Wurmple on Route 101 — free Bug coverage; evolves random into Beautifly or Dustox
  • Run the bag-grab tutorial in Petalburg Woods for free Potions
  • Talk to your dad in Petalburg Gym — he sets up HM01 dialogue (badge comes later)
Worth doing
  • Save before Birch’s nature-quiz boy if you’re hunting a specific personality
  • Talk to every NPC in Littleroot — the Pokemon Center clerk teaches healing
  • Catch Zigzagoon for Pickup ability (random items between battles)
Phase 02 · Petalburg Woods → Rustboro

Hour one to two.

The first stretch of real progression. Petalburg Woods hides the run-defining catch. Rustboro is the first gym, the Devon errand, and your first taste of Aqua/Magma.

Catch a Shroomish in Petalburg Woods. It evolves into Breloom and learns Mach Punch — a priority Fighting move — at Lv 23. Mach Punch trivializes Roxanne’s Geodudes and Norman’s Slaking. There is no better single catch you can make in the first hour.

Battle every trainer you pass. Emerald’s EXP curve is steep early, and skipped trainer EXP can desync you for the next two gyms. Stock 5–10 Potions and 3–5 Antidotes before you enter the woods — Wurmples are everywhere and Poison Sting tags accumulate fast.

Money tip

Save your money. Poke Balls cost 200 each but the Devon Goods sidequest hands you five free Premier Balls on delivery.

Must do
  • Catch Shroomish in Petalburg Woods — Mach Punch at Lv 23 wins gyms 1 and 5
  • Battle every trainer for EXP — the early curve punishes skips
  • Buy 5–10 Potions, 3–5 Antidotes before the woods
Worth doing
  • Wait on Poke Balls — 5 free Premier Balls come from the Devon errand
  • Pick up the Miracle Seed in Petalburg Woods — +10% Grass damage for Treecko
  • Talk to the Cuttable-tree NPC by Rustboro for HM01 prep
Phase 03 · Dewford & Granite Cave

Hour two to three.

Mr. Briney sails you across to Dewford. Inside Granite Cave, the original Champion is hiding behind a wall of Whismurs — and so is your future Aggron.

Catch Aron on Granite Cave 1F. It evolves into Lairon at Lv 32 and Aggron at Lv 42 — a massive Steel/Rock wall with Sturdy or Rock Head. Don’t waste Repels in Granite Cave; the encounter rate for Aron is decent and you specifically want it.

Talk to Steven in the deepest part of the cave. He’s the OG Champion (Wallace took over in Emerald) and trades you TM47 Steel Wing for delivering the Devon Goods. Steel Wing is a clean Skarmory/Aron move and is foreshadowed throughout the rest of the game.

Field tip

After Steel Wing, sail back to Slateport to deliver the Devon Goods and unlock Cycling Road. Rydel’s Cycles is the next stop.

Must do
  • Catch Aron on Granite Cave 1F — future Aggron
  • Talk to Steven in the cave’s lowest level for TM47 Steel Wing
  • Beat Brawly with Wing Attack / Confusion / Dustox Gust
  • Sail back to Slateport with Devon Goods to open Cycling Road
Worth doing
  • Skip Repels in Granite Cave — you want the Aron encounters
  • Pick up the Everstone in Granite Cave for breeding
  • Talk to the Trick Master’s Route 110 sign — first puzzle unlocks here
Phase 04 · Mauville & beyond

Hour three to four.

Mauville opens up Hoenn. Rydel hands you a bike, Wattson hands you a badge, and the Game Corner hands you Thunderbolt — if you’ve got the patience.

Pick up the Mach Bike or Acro Bike at Rydel’s Cycles. Mach Bike is needed for Mirage Tower (Anorith / Lileep) and Sky Pillar; Acro Bike unlocks Trick House quirks and certain Secret Base spots. You can swap any time by returning to Rydel — pick the one your next destination needs.

Catch Electrike on Route 110. It evolves into Manectric, one of the best Electric types in Emerald and a one-Pokemon answer to Winona and Wallace. After Wattson, talk to him again to start the New Mauville generator quest — the reward is TM24 Thunderbolt for free.

Money tip

If you’re patient, the Mauville Game Corner sells TM24 Thunderbolt for 4000 Coins. The cherry/replay slot strategy is grindy but legitimate.

Must do
  • Get a bike at Rydel’s Cycles — Mach for terrain, Acro for tricks
  • Catch Electrike on Route 110 for Manectric
  • Beat Wattson, then go back for the New Mauville generator quest
  • After Dynamo Badge: Rock Smash boulders for items, Geodude, Nosepass
Worth doing
  • Game Corner slots if you’re patient — TM24 Thunderbolt for 4000 Coins
  • First Trick House puzzle (Route 110) for the Hard Stone
  • Trade Pokemon at the Mauville name-rater for nicknames
  • Save before Wattson — Voltorb’s Self-Destruct can clip a low-HP party member
Phase 05 · Verdanturf · Mt. Chimney · Lavaridge

Hour four to five.

The desert opens up. Mirage Tower asks you to choose a fossil — one or the other, never both. Then Mt. Chimney erupts into the first major team-plot fight, and Lavaridge gives you a free egg.

Mirage Tower in the desert requires the Mach Bike and Go-Goggles. Inside, you choose ONE fossil: Root Fossil for Lileep / Cradily (tanky Grass/Rock) or Claw Fossil for Anorith / Armaldo (offensive Bug/Rock). The other crumbles into the sand — permanently. Pick Anorith if your team is light on a Rock attacker; Lileep if you need a wall.

After climbing Mt. Chimney, you fight Team Magma at the summit. Heal first — the staircase is long and there’s no Pokemon Center between Fiery Path and the peak. Lavaridge gives you a free Wynaut egg from the old woman near the hot springs; Wobbuffet has utility in doubles and the Frontier.

Free heals

The Lavaridge Hot Springs fully heal your team for free — faster than the Pokemon Center if you’re grinding nearby. The old woman gives you the Wynaut egg here.

Must do
  • Choose a fossil in Mirage Tower — Root or Claw, never both
  • Heal before the Mt. Chimney summit fight
  • Pick up the Wynaut egg from the Lavaridge hot springs
  • Defeat Flannery with Marshtomp / Vibrava / Wailmer’s Surf
Worth doing
  • Use the Hot Springs as a free Pokemon Center while grinding
  • TM50 Overheat is Flannery’s drop — great on Camerupt or Blaziken
  • Talk to every Verdanturf NPC — one teaches Tickle for breeding-only utility
  • Catch Numel on Route 112 for a future Camerupt and Magnitude EQ
