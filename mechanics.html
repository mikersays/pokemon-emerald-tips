Systems under the surface

The hidden
mechanics.

Six systems Emerald never explains in dialogue: the Gen 3 type chart and its quirks, the seven status conditions and their counters, the abilities that change battles outright, the 25 natures and which ones to chase, the friendship value behind every level-up, and the catch-rate math behind every Pokeball. The encyclopedia entry for trainers who want to understand why their plans worked or didn’t.

Section I

The Gen 3
type chart

Seventeen types. Fairy does not exist yet — it arrived in Gen 6 and reshaped everything. Steel is the most resistant defensive type in Gen 3 (eleven resistances and one immunity), and it resists Ghost and Dark specifically — both nerfed in Gen 6. Most other interactions you remember from later games still apply, with three sharp exceptions worth memorizing.

All 17 types
normal fire water grass electric ice fighting poison ground flying psychic bug rock ghost dragon dark steel
Quirk 01

Steel resists Ghost AND Dark

Changed in Gen 6. In Gen 3, Steel is the most resistant defensive type — 11 resistances plus one immunity (Poison). Skarmory, Aggron, and Metagross are anchors.

Quirk 02

Ghost is immune to Normal AND Fighting

Dual immunity. Hariyama with Brick Break does zero damage to a Gengar. Use Crunch (Dark) or Shadow Ball (Ghost) instead.

Gengar · the textbook Ghost wall
Quirk 03

Dark is immune to Psychic

Sableye and Sharpedo eat Psychic moves. Tate & Liza’s gym is hard-countered by Sharpedo — Crunch one-shots both aces.

Sableye & Sharpedo · psychic-immune
Quirk 04

Bug is super effective vs Dark, Psychic, Grass

Heracross’s Megahorn is a top-tier nuke vs Slowking, Gardevoir, and Sidney’s entire team. Bug is the most overlooked offensive type in Gen 3.

Heracross · Megahorn nuke
Quirk 05

Dragon is super effective ONLY vs Dragon

No Fairy resistance yet. Outrage from Salamence walls everything that doesn’t resist or immune. Steel resists Dragon — the safest defensive answer.

Salamence · the dragon ceiling
Quirk 06

Levitate beats Ground

Electric is immune to Ground. But Levitate ability (Flygon, Gengar, Claydol) makes them immune to Ground regardless of typing. Magneton is Steel/Electric so it’s still hit by Ground.

Magneton · not immune (Steel/Electric)
Dual-type calculation

Type effectiveness multiplies. A 2× weakness on one type and a 2× weakness on the other = 4×. A 2× weakness and a 0.5× resist = 1× neutral. Salamence (Dragon/Flying) hit by Ice = 4×. Swampert (Water/Ground) hit by Grass = 4× — ONLY weakness. Skarmory (Steel/Flying) hit by Rock = 1× neutral, NOT 2×.

The Lum Berry rule

Lum Berry cures any status condition the Pokemon currently has — including confusion and infatuation — in one held-item slot. Replant Lum Berries on Soft Soil patches; harvest 2–4 per tree. Pre-load every Frontier team with Lum Berries.

Section IV

All 25
natures

25 natures total. 5 are neutral (no stat effect): Hardy, Docile, Serious, Bashful, Quirky. The other 20 boost one stat 10% and reduce another 10%. Natures are randomly assigned at generation/encounter and never change.

The 5 neutral natures

Hardy (Atk-Atk), Docile (Def-Def), Serious (Spe-Spe), Bashful (SpA-SpA), Quirky (SpD-SpD). All five do nothing — useful only for collectors and balanced unrestricted play.

Synchronize + Everstone protocol

For breeding: female parent holding Everstone passes Nature 50% of the time. For wild captures (legendaries especially): lead with a Synchronize Ralts of your target Nature for 50% encounter match. Stack both for the most reliable Gen 3 Nature lock.

No Nature in summary screen

Gen 3 Emerald does NOT explicitly display Nature in the summary screen. To check, look at level-1 base stats — the boosted stat will show numerically higher than baseline. Diamond/Pearl onwards added Nature with color-coded stat indicators. To check via stats: hatch the egg, compare Lv 1 stats to species base values, the +10%/−10% becomes obvious.

Section V

Friendship
& happiness

A hidden 0–255 value per Pokemon. Controls Return move power, Frustration move power, and friendship-based evolutions. Most Pokemon start at 70.

The cartridge tracks your bond with every Pokemon as a number you cannot see in-game. Walking, leveling, and vitamins all add to it. Fainting and bitter herbal items subtract. The value drives two moves (Return and Frustration) and gates five evolutions (Crobat, Pikachu, Marill, Jigglypuff, Clefairy from their baby forms).

The fastest path to maxing friendship: Luxury Ball capture (2× gains) + Soothe Bell hold (1.5×, stacks). Walk constantly with the Pokemon in party, level up often, feed stat-lowering reset berries. Crobat in <30 minutes of optimized play.

Return (TM27)

Power = friendship / 2.5. Max 102 BP at 255 friendship. Best move for high-friendship physical attackers like Vigoroth, Linoone, Crobat.

Frustration (TM21)

Power = (255 − friendship) / 2.5. Inverse of Return. Use with low-friendship Pokemon. Most playthroughs ignore this.

No checker NPC

There is NO friendship-checker NPC in Emerald (added Gen 4 — Pokemon Massage Lady). Player must intuit friendship from move power scaling.

Section VII

Damage, crits
& priority

The math under every hit. Gen 3 critical hits double damage AND ignore beneficial defensive buffs — not the 1.5x of Gen 6+. Move priority brackets settle ties. Weather doubles or halves whole types.

Formula

The simplified Gen 3 damage formula

damage = ((((2 × level / 5 + 2) × BasePower × Atk / Def) / 50) + 2)
    × STAB × TypeEff × Burn × Random(0.85–1.0)

  • STAB = 1.5× if move type matches user’s type, else 1.0×
  • TypeEff = 0 / 0.25 / 0.5 / 1 / 2 / 4 (multiplicative on dual-type)
  • Burn = 0.5× if attacker is burned and the move is Physical (Gen 3 splits damage class by TYPE: Fire/Water/Grass/Electric/Ice/Psychic/Dark/Dragon = Special; Normal/Fighting/Poison/Ground/Flying/Bug/Rock/Ghost/Steel = Physical)
  • Random = 85–100% per hit (±15% spread)
Crit math

Gen 3 crits: 2× & ignore buffs

Quirk

2× damage, not 1.5×

Gen 3 crits deal double damage (Gen 6+ nerfed to 1.5×). Crits IGNORE positive Def/SpD on the target (Light Screen, Reflect, Cosmic Power) AND ignore negative Atk/SpA on the user (Burn’s halved Atk, Intimidate). Still respect STAB, type, and weather.

Field tip

Mach Punch on Breloom (+1 priority, 40 BP STAB) outspeeds anything in its bracket, fixing Breloom’s 70 base Speed. Quick Attack on Crobat / Linoone for clean revenge KOs.

Multi-target damage in doubles

Moves that hit multiple targets deal 50% damage to each. Surf 95 BP → ~47.5 BP per target. Earthquake 100 BP → 50 BP per target AND hits allies (use a Levitate / Flying / Wonder Guard partner to spam Quake safely). Explosion / Self-Destruct halve too. Rock Slide is single-target hitting two — full damage on each.

Berries

Auto-cure berries

Cheri (paralysis), Chesto (sleep), Pecha (poison), Rawst (burn), Aspear (freeze), Persim (confusion).

Pinch berries (HP < 25%)

Liechi +1 Atk · Ganlon +1 Def · Salac +1 Spe · Petaya +1 SpA · Apicot +1 SpD · Lansat +1 crit stage · Starf +2 random stat. All trigger at HP < 25%. Endure + Salac on a fast Pokemon is a classic Frontier sweep setup. Pinch berries are RARE — Berry Master daily phrases or Mirage Island Liechi.

Sitrus / Oran proc threshold

Sitrus heals 30 HP flat at <50% HP. Oran heals 10 HP flat at <50%. Sitrus is strictly better — plant once, harvest forever.

Closing transmission

Six systems. One cartridge.

Type chart, status, abilities, natures, friendship, balls. Read them once. Memorize the quirks: Steel resists Ghost and Dark, Ground hits Levitate as zero, paralysis halves Speed by four, sleep is 1–7 turns. The rest falls out from there.
