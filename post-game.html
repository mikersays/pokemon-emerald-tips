Chapter 09 · After Wallace

The true
endgame of Hoenn

The credits are a comma, not a period. After Hall of Fame, board the ship at Lilycove and the Battle Frontier opens — seven facilities, seven Brains, fourteen Symbols. Below: every facility, the gimmick that defines it, the legendaries that respawn, and the rematch most players never find.

The Endgame Engine

The Battle
Frontier

Reachable by ferry from Lilycove once you’ve cleared the Hall of Fame, the Battle Frontier is an artificial island built around competitive battling. Seven facilities, each run by a Frontier Brain, each earning you a Silver Symbol on first clear and a Gold Symbol on second — fourteen total. The currency is BP (Battle Points), earned per win streak; spend it on rare items, EV-reset berries, and move tutors. Most facilities offer Open Level (your team as-is) and Level 50 (auto-scaled) modes — pick whichever your roster fits.

House Rules

No items in battle — you cannot use Potions, Revives, or Full Restores from your bag inside any Frontier facility. Held items (Leftovers, Choice Band, Lum Berry) still work. Two facilities (Battle Factory, Battle Pyramid) hand you rental Pokemon or strip your held items entirely — check the per-facility brief before entering.

Location

Artificial island, ferry from Lilycove Harbor, post-Hall of Fame.

Currency

Battle Points (BP) earned per win streak; spent at the Frontier shops.

Symbols

14 total — one Silver, one Gold, per facility. Brain rematches scale.

Roster of Brains

The seven
facilities

Each Brain runs a single facility with a single rule. Read the gimmick before you challenge — Battle Palace, in particular, will lose for you if you don’t pre-tune your team’s Natures.

No. 01 · Ability Symbol A psychic

Battle Tower

Brain · Anabel, the Salon Maiden

Format
Choose 3 Pokemon, win 7 in a row, repeat the cycle.
Level
Lv 50 (auto) or Open Lv 100
Gimmick
Standard 3v3 singles. Pure endurance test — the only variable is your patience.
Symbol
Silver Ability at 49 wins · Gold at 70 wins
Silver team
Alakazam + Snorlax + Entei
Gold team
Raikou + Latios + Snorlax
Strategy. A balanced trio is the only durable answer: a physical wall, a special wall, and a sweeper.
  • Skarmory or Suicune for physical bulk & Toxic stalling.
  • Snorlax or Blissey/Chansey-equivalent for special bulk.
  • A Choice-Band sweeper or Calm Mind setup user as the closer.
  • Avoid gimmick movesets — the AI will outlast cute strategies.
No. 02 · Tactics Symbol T fire

Battle Dome

Brain · Tucker, the Dome Ace

Format
Tournament bracket, 16 trainers, 2v2 elimination.
Gimmick
You can scout enemy Pokemon stats and moves before each match.
Watch out
The AI judges victory partly on damage-dealt — OHKOs win even tied matches.
Symbol
Silver Tactics at first clear · Gold at the rematch
Brain ace
Salamence · Tucker’s closer
Strategy. The format favors quick OHKOs over long grinds.
  • Choice Band attackers shine here — Salamence, Metagross, Heracross.
  • Use the scout screen religiously: pick the move that one-shots the lead.
  • Avoid stall builds — the bracket is short and damage scoring matters.
  • Tucker’s ace is Salamence; pack an Ice Beam or Stone Edge user.
No. 03 · Spirits Symbol S grass

Battle Palace

Brain · Spenser, the Palace Maven

Format
3v3 single — YOU don’t pick moves. Pokemon Nature decides each turn.
Gimmick
Aggressive natures attack; defensive ones heal/buff. RNG-heavy.
Watch out
This is the most frustrating facility in the game — randomness rules.
Symbol
Silver Spirits at 35 wins · Gold at 70
Brain ace
Slaking · Spenser’s heavy
Strategy. Pre-tune your roster: pick recipients with single-purpose movesets and aggressive natures so the random selection still does work.
  • Adamant/Modest natures with one strong STAB move — deny the AI bad choices.
  • Skip status moves entirely; they roll defensively and waste turns.
  • Hard-hitting OHKO Pokemon (Snorlax with Body Slam, Salamence with Outrage) ride the dice.
  • Bring Lum Berry — status from opponents will paralyze your auto-AI for free turns.
No. 04 · Guts Symbol G fighting

Battle Arena

Brain · Greta, the Arena Tycoon

Format
1v1 with a 3-turn judging system. After 3 turns, judges score Mind/Skill/Body.
Gimmick
Switching is banned. Status moves are penalized in scoring.
Quirk
Slaking shines here — Truant doesn’t dock the score the way status moves do.
Symbol
Silver Guts at 28 wins · Gold at 56
Brain ace
Heracross · Greta’s lead
Strategy. Offensive natures and STAB moves only. The judges punish hesitation.
  • Hariyama with Cross Chop, Brick Break, Knock Off, Rock Slide is the canonical pick.
  • Slaking with Hyper Beam / Earthquake / Shadow Ball / Return overpowers the 3-turn limit.
  • Avoid setup moves like Bulk Up — they cost you Mind score.
  • Bring a Lum Berry — one Hypnosis from a Lunatone ends a run.
No. 05 · Knowledge Symbol N steel

Battle Factory

Brain · Noland, the Factory Head

Format
Rentals — you get a random pool, choose 3, swap with defeated opponents’ Pokemon each round.
Gimmick
You don’t use your own Pokemon at all. Every Pokemon is held-itemless.
Watch out
Movesets are random per rental; a Choice-locked Pokemon can wreck a run.
Symbol
Silver Knowledge at 21 wins · Gold at 42
Brain ace
Articuno · Noland’s rental
Strategy. Knowledge of base stats and movesets is the entire facility.
  • Round 1: pick a balanced typing trio — cover Fire/Water/Grass yourself.
  • Always swap into a Pokemon that covers your previous loss’s weakness.
  • Memorize ace tiers: Salamence, Metagross, Tyranitar are upper-tier rentals worth holding.
  • Avoid setup-dependent rentals (no Curse Snorlax without Rest support in the moveset).
No. 06 · Luck Symbol L poison

Battle Pike

Brain · Lucy, the Pike Queen

Format
Pick a path through 14 rooms: trainers, wild Pokemon, status traps, NPCs.
Gimmick
Lottery-style; pick from 3 doors blind. Status floors can wipe a run.
Watch out
Lucy’s ace is Seviper with Swagger + Toxic — bring a Lum Berry.
Symbol
Silver Luck at 28 rooms · Gold at 140
Brain ace
Seviper · Lucy’s ace
Strategy. Build for survivability over offense.
  • Bring a tank with Rest or Recover — Suicune, Milotic, or Blissey-class.
  • Lum Berry on every Pokemon — status traps are the most common run-ending event.
  • Hint signs near doors leak the next room’s contents — read every NPC dialogue.
  • Prefer the “safer” door even if the prize seems smaller; consistency beats peaks.
No. 07 · Brave Symbol B ghost

Battle Pyramid

Brain · Brandon, the Pyramid King

Format
7-floor dark maze. Wild Pokemon, trainers, items. Find the boss with limited Flash range.
Gimmick
Held items don’t carry between floors — you find new ones in the maze.
Watch out
Brandon’s gold rematch uses the Regis (Regirock, Regice, Registeel).
Symbol
Silver Brave at 21 floors · Gold at 70
Silver team
Regi trio · Lv 50
Gold team
Kanto bird trio · Lv 50
Strategy. Endurance, navigation, and HP-recovery built-in.
  • Pokemon with high HP and Leftovers (found mid-maze) self-recover between fights.
  • Memorize floor-tile patterns — the maze layouts repeat across runs.
  • Prioritize Pokemon that learn Flash naturally so you don’t waste an item slot.
  • Kingdra (Hydro Pump, Ice Beam, Surf) handles all three Regis with neutral coverage.
Spend the Symbols

Clarification

Emerald does NOT have the Sevii Islands — that’s a FireRed/LeafGreen feature. Emerald’s post-game equivalents are the Battle Frontier, Trainer Hill, and the legendary trio quest (Regis via the Sealed Chamber, roaming Latios/Latias, and the weather trio). If a guide tells you to sail to One Island, you’re reading FireRed by mistake.

The Cable Car Tower

Trainer Hill

Mauville — Verdanturf cable car route

Four floors. Sixteen trainers. One stopwatch.

Trainer Hill is a four-floor trainer-rush facility tucked alongside the Mauville-to-Verdanturf cable car route. The structure is fixed: 4 floors x 4 trainers each = 16 fights per run. The theme you pick on the receptionist screen swaps the trainer roster but not the floor count. The entire run is timed; records save per theme.

Theme: Variety

Default theme. Mixed trainer types, balanced movesets. Best for first clears and the casual time-trial floor (sub-15 minute target).

Theme: Unique

Gimmick teams — mono-type trainers, status-heavy squads, double-battle pairs. Great EV practice; awkward for raw speed.

Theme: Strong

Highest-level rosters, EV-trained-feel movesets. The serious time-trial theme; sub-10 minutes is the speedrun benchmark.

Theme: Expert

Unlocks after a Strong-theme clear. Mirrors Frontier-tier movesets, includes Lum Berries on most leads. The hardest tier.

Rewards. No money or items per trainer — the prize is your time-stamp record, saved per theme. The Hill is a self-contained challenge, not a grind spot.

FIELD TIP — Trainer Hill rewards in-and-out efficiency, not raw power. A fast sweeper with Earthquake + Aerial Ace burns through floors faster than a tanky Frontier squad.

PokeNav & the Phone Tree

Gym & trainer rematches

What scales, what doesn’t

Hoenn was the cartridge before rematches were standard.

Two things to know before you start grinding rematches:

  • Gym Leader rematches are NOT in vanilla Emerald. They were added retroactively in Omega Ruby/Alpha Sapphire. The HeartGold/SoulSilver phone-call rematch system isn’t here either. If you want gym-leader rematches, you need ORAS.
  • Regular trainers across Hoenn DO rematch. Many of them ask for your PokeNav number during the original fight. After Hall of Fame, they’ll call you (or you can call them) and offer rematches with stronger teams.
  • Post-Hall-of-Fame trainer rematches give the largest single-fight EXP in the game — better than most Frontier streaks for raw level grinding.
Route 111 north house

Winstrate Family (Vito + 4)

5 trainers in a row, repeatable

The most efficient grind on the cartridge. Five trainers in a row, near a Pokemon Center, all offer rematches with scaling teams. Heal between Vito and the rest by stepping outside.

Route 121 (between Mt. Pyre & Lilycove)

Gentleman Walter

Highest cash payout per rematch

Pays the most cash of any single trainer. Only one Pokemon per fight, so it’s a clean burst — but the cooldown means you cycle other rematches between his calls.

Route 113 (volcanic ash route)

Pokemaniac Steve

Numel/Camerupt lover; high EXP

Multiple Numel/Camerupt rematches at scaling levels. Drops Sitrus Berries on rare RNG. Park your EV grinder on Route 113 between cycles.

Route 117 (cycling road south)

Lass Kayla

Nugget farm, low frequency

Holds Nuggets that her Pokemon drop in rematches at low rate. Good for the Nugget-shop side grind; bad for raw EXP.

Route 111 (desert south)

Beauty Connie

Skitty/Delcatty — Cute Charm risk

Cash + EV chip from Skitty/Delcatty rematches. Watch for Cute Charm infatuation; bring a same-gender attacker.

Route 110 (Mauville south)

Cooltrainer Wilton

Strong team, near Mauville Pokemon Center

Tougher rematch — full team, balanced movesets, decent payout. Mauville is across the bridge for instant resupply.

Mt. Pyre exterior

Hex Maniac Patricia

Ghost rematches; Black Sludge chance

Ghost-type rematches with low-frequency Black Sludge and Spell Tag drops. Niche, but a useful stop on the Mt. Pyre EV route.

PokeNav meta

Match Call tracking

Green-dot icons = ready

Open PokeNav → Match Call. Trainers ready for rematch show a green dot. Cycle by waiting in-game time, biking off-and-on the route, or quick-flying away and back.

Field technique

The Repel trick for roamers

Latios / Latias / wild Lv 40 roamers

Lock the encounter slot.

Latios and Latias roam Hoenn after the news event. They appear once per route then teleport on the next encounter check. Chasing them by random walking burns hours; the Repel trick reduces the search to one accurate encounter per route.

  1. Slot 1. Place a Lv 41+ Pokemon at the head of your party. Repel blocks all wild encounters of LOWER level than your lead, so the Lv 40 Lati becomes the only legal hit.
  2. Track. PokeNav → Match Call → the roaming-marker map. The Lati’s location plots as a flashing icon.
  3. Position. Fly to a route adjacent to the marker, walk in.
  4. Save first. If the Lati flees on turn 1, the marker JUMPS to a new route. Save BEFORE entering so soft-reset puts the roamer back where you found it.
  5. Lock. Mean Look (Crobat, Lv 30+ via tutor) traps it. Master Ball or Net-Ball-after-Mean-Look is your shot.

FIELD TIP — The same trick works for the post-game wild legendaries that respawn at Lv 70 (Groudon/Kyogre/Rayquaza). Lv 71+ lead + Repel = clean encounter.

The Long Tail

Completion goals

Five goals separate “beat the game” from “cleared the cartridge.” None are mandatory; each takes 20+ hours.

Endgame checklist
  • Catch all 386 Pokemon. Requires trades with FireRed/LeafGreen and Ruby/Sapphire to fill the National Pokedex. Several Pokemon are unobtainable without those carts.
  • Earn all 14 Battle Frontier Symbols. Both Silver and Gold from each of the seven Brains. Brandon’s Gold is the gauntlet — he uses the Regi trio.
  • Master all 5 Contest categories. Cool, Beauty, Cute, Smart, Tough — clear Master Rank in each. Feebas evolves into Milotic via this loop, so it’s an integrated quest.
  • Beat Trainer Hill in record time. Sub-15 on Variety is the casual goal; speedrunners chase the Strong-theme world record.
  • Find Mirage Island. The mythical island only appears when your party’s personality value matches the day’s seed — roughly 1-in-65,536 odds. The Wynaut you can catch there is purely cosmetic; the real reward is the boast.
Final word

The Battle Frontier is the test the rest of Hoenn was preparing you for. If you grew up with this cartridge and never finished a Gold Symbol, this is the year you go back. Bring a Skarmory.
