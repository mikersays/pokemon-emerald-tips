of Hoenn

No. 252 · Hoenn Pokedex

Treecko

Treecko → Grovyle (Lv 16) → Sceptile (Lv 36)

grass Speed · Sp. Atk

“Rewarding but punishing — 6/10 for new players.”

Strengths
  • Fastest of the three starters at every stage of the run.
  • Strong against Roxanne, Wattson (with Bullet Seed), Wallace, and Drake’s Kingdra.
  • Learns Leaf Blade naturally as Grovyle (Lv 29) — one of the best moves in Gen 3.
  • Pure Grass typing: only one weakness multiplier per super-effective hit.
Weaknesses
  • Bad matchup against Flannery, Winona, Phoebe, Glacia, and most of the Elite Four.
  • Off-type movepool is shallow on the cartridge — mostly Crunch and Dragon Claw via TM.
  • Frail defensive stats; stray Ice or Flying moves can one-shot.

Trivia

Sceptile was the first pure Grass starter final with a base 120 Speed stat, and its design was inspired by the Carolina anole lizard. In Gen 6 it gained a Mega Evolution that turned it Grass/Dragon — vindicating every trainer who put Dragon Claw on theirs.

No. 255 · Hoenn Pokedex

Torchic

Torchic → Combusken (Lv 16, +Fighting) → Blaziken (Lv 36)

fire fighting Mixed Atk

“The easiest run in Emerald — 9/10 for new players.”

Strengths
  • Becomes Fire/Fighting at Combusken — incredible coverage for a 4th-badge run.
  • Double Kick at Lv 16 trivializes Roxanne’s gym.
  • Sky Uppercut as Combusken hits Phoebe, Sidney, and Glacia for super-effective damage.
  • Blaze Kick + Sky Uppercut covers most of Hoenn’s threats with two STABs.
Weaknesses
  • Slow until Blaziken; outsped by many gym aces in the mid-game.
  • Awful matchup against Wallace and Tate & Liza’s Lunatone.
  • No 4× weakness, but stacks Water, Ground, Flying, and Psychic vulnerabilities.

Trivia

Blaziken was the first Fire/Fighting starter, kicking off a Gen-long tradition (Infernape, Emboar, Cinderace). It also became the first Pokemon ever given a Mega Evolution in X/Y — a quiet acknowledgment of how good it was on the cartridge.

No. 258 · Hoenn Pokedex

Mudkip

Mudkip → Marshtomp (Lv 16, +Ground) → Swampert (Lv 36)

water ground Atk · HP · Bulk

“Statistically the easiest run — 10/10.”

Strengths
  • Becomes Water/Ground at Marshtomp — immune to Electric, only one weakness (4× Grass).
  • Solos Roxanne, Wattson, Flannery, and Tate & Liza without breaking a sweat.
  • Highest bulk of any starter; ideal for new trainers learning the systems.
  • Earthquake, Surf, and Ice Beam by TM give full coverage out of the box.
Weaknesses
  • 4× weak to Grass — Drake’s Salamence and wild Ludicolo can sting.
  • Slowest of the three starters; rarely outspeeds gym aces.
  • Predictable; many trainers carry Razor Leaf or Giga Drain.

Trivia

Swampert is one of only three Water/Ground families in Gen 3 (with Whiscash and Quagsire). The “So I herd u liek mudkipz” meme originated from a 2007 4chan thread and helped cement Mudkip in pop culture — long after the cartridge stopped selling.
