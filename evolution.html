When B is your best friend

The art of
evolving deliberately.

Six mechanics, one B button. Gen 3 Emerald supports level-up, stone, trade, friendship, beauty (contest stat), and conditional evolutions — and lets you delay every one of them. Underneath: a Personality Value the cartridge rolls on capture and never tells you about, branching outcomes baked in from the moment a Wurmple appears.

Section I

Six
methods

Most Hoenn Pokemon evolve by hitting a level threshold and triggering at end of battle. The rest demand a specific item, an action, a contest stat, or a trade with another cartridge. Knowing which method applies saves you from holding a Leaf Stone for a Pokemon that wants a Sun Stone.

Method 01 · Most common L level-up

Level-up

End-of-battle trigger · species-specific level

Hits a target level and evolves automatically at end of battle. Cancellable by holding B during the animation.

Treecko → Lv 16
Zubat → Lv 22
Magikarp → Lv 20
How it fires. The end-of-battle exp jingle ends, then any Pokemon at or above its evolution level checks. If you EXP-share into the threshold, the check still fires when the battle ends.

FIELD TIP — Rare Candy triggers level-up checks. Hold B to cancel if you’re still grinding pre-evo moves.

Method 02 · Item-driven S stone

Evolution Stone

Use from bag · instant trigger, consumes the stone

Use a stone from the bag — instant evolution, no level requirement above the species minimum. Stones are consumed. Save the bag inventory before applying, especially with Eevee.

Vulpix + Fire
Gloom + Leaf
Eevee + Water
What stones do. Bypass any level requirement above the species’ floor. The stone is consumed regardless of whether the resulting form is what you wanted — soft-reset before applying to a one-of-a-kind Pokemon.
Method 03 · Cable-cabled T trade

Trade

Link cable required · evolves on receipt

Trade the Pokemon over a link cable. It evolves on receipt by the new trainer. NOT possible single-cart — you need a partner Game Boy / cable.

Kadabra → Alakazam
Machoke → Machamp
Graveler → Golem
Haunter → Gengar
The single-cart problem. Without a second GBA + Emerald/Ruby/Sapphire/FireRed/LeafGreen and a link cable, these Pokemon stay in their pre-evolved form forever. The easiest single-player path is two GBAs and a link cable, even if both carts are yours.
Method 04 · Hidden value F friendship

Friendship

Hidden 0–255 value · level up at ≥ 220

Level up while friendship value is ≥ 220 (out of 255). Soothe Bell speeds this up by 1.5×; Luxury Ball doubles all gains. Stack both for ~3× speed.

Golbat → Crobat
Pichu → Pikachu
Azurill → Marill
Method 05 · Contest-only B beauty

Beauty (contest stat)

Unique to Feebas in Emerald

Level up while contest Beauty stat is sufficiently high. The only path to Milotic in vanilla Emerald. Feed Dry-flavored Pokeblocks; max Beauty; level once; transform.

Feebas
→
Milotic
Method 06 · Personality Value P branched

Branched / PV-determined

32-bit hidden number set at encounter or hatch

Outcome is determined by the Pokemon’s hidden Personality Value — set at encounter or hatch and never changed. The most famous instance is Wurmple’s split (see Section III).

Nincada deserves a special mention: Nincada → Ninjask at Lv 20, AND Shedinja appears in your party box if you have an empty slot and a spare Pokeball. The PV is not the gate; the empty slot is. Most other branched evolutions in later gens (e.g., Salandit, Wurmple's PV cousins) inherit the same logic.

Nincada / Ninjask / Shedinja
Section II

Delaying
evolution

When the evolution animation starts, hold the B button until the “Huh?” message displays. The Pokemon stays in its current form. The next time it levels up, the prompt fires again. Below: seven specific Emerald lines where delaying earns you a real movepool advantage.

The principle

Most evolved forms learn moves later — or never — compared to their pre-evolutions. Hold off on stones and trade evolutions until you’ve gotten the moves you want. B-cancel level evolutions until the desired level is past.

Line

Gloom → Vileplume

Gloom learns Petal Dance at Lv 39 and Moonlight at Lv 49. Vileplume’s level-up pool stops at Aromatherapy. Waiting on the Leaf Stone is correct.

DELAY UNTIL — Lv 49 (or Lv 39 if you can live without Moonlight).

Line

Vibrava → Flygon

Vibrava learns Dragonbreath at Lv 35; Flygon does NOT learn this naturally (only via tutor in later games). Hit Lv 35 as Vibrava before evolving at Lv 45.

DELAY UNTIL — Lv 35.

Line

Eevee → Eeveelution

Eevee learns Bite (Lv 16) and Take Down (Lv 30). Eeveelutions get type-specific elemental attacks but lose the Normal moves. Decide your build before stoning.

DELAY UNTIL — You’ve picked up the moves you want.

Line

Kirlia → Gardevoir

Both forms learn the same level-up set (Calm Mind, Future Sight, Hypnosis line). Evolve at Lv 30 freely — no movepool penalty.

EVOLVE IMMEDIATELY — the exception.

Line

Nuzleaf → Shiftry

Nuzleaf learns Extrasensory at Lv 39 by level-up; Shiftry doesn’t level-up learn this. Don’t use the Leaf Stone before Lv 39.

DELAY UNTIL — Lv 39.

Line

Pikachu → Raichu

Pikachu learns Thunder at Lv 50; Raichu does NOT learn Thunder by level-up. If you don’t have TM25, keep Pikachu until Lv 50.

DELAY UNTIL — Lv 50 (no TM25).

Line

Magikarp → Gyarados

Magikarp’s pool is famously useless (Splash, Tackle, Flail) — evolve immediately at Lv 20. The exception that proves the rule.

EVOLVE IMMEDIATELY — nothing to wait for.

Line

Dragonair → Dragonite

Not native to Hoenn (FRLG/Colosseum import). Dragonair learns Outrage at Lv 51 vs Dragonite at Lv 61 — delay 10 levels for Outrage.

DELAY UNTIL — Lv 51.

Section III

The Wurmple
branch

The myth: Wurmple’s evolution is rolled when it evolves. The truth: it was decided the moment you encountered it.

Wurmple · PV-locked W bug

Wurmple

Personality Value branch · ((PV >> 16) % 10)

Wurmple
→
Silcoon (PV%10 < 5)
→
Beautifly
Wurmple
→
Cascoon (PV%10 ≥ 5)
→
Dustox
The truth. Wurmple’s evolution path is determined by its 32-bit Personality Value, set when the Wurmple is generated. The relevant byte: ((PV >> 16) % 10). If 0–4, Silcoon → Beautifly. If 5–9, Cascoon → Dustox.

Implication. If you save before evolving and reset, you’ll always get the same outcome — the Wurmple was “fated” from capture. To get the other branch, catch a different Wurmple.

FIELD TIP — Hatching eggs from a Wurmple parent and checking at Lv 7 cocoon stage is faster than catching repeatedly.

Eevee is not obtainable in vanilla Emerald wild. Trade from Pokemon FireRed or LeafGreen (Celadon Game Corner reward, 4900 coins) or from Pokemon XD/Colosseum.

Section V

Trade
evolutions

Twelve trade evos exist within the Hoenn dex’s trade-evolution roster. Without a second cart and a link cable, every one of them stays in its pre-evolved form forever — the most painful single-cart limitation Emerald has.

01 · Any trade

Kadabra → Alakazam

Plain trade evo. Both share movepool; trading early is fine. Synchronize lead works on either.

02 · Any trade

Machoke → Machamp

Plain trade evo. Massive Atk jump — one of the cleanest trade-locked sweepers.

03 · Any trade

Graveler → Golem

Plain trade evo. Sturdy line, tanky Rock/Ground — key counter to Wattson without Marshtomp.

04 · Any trade

Haunter → Gengar

Plain trade evo. Levitate, Hypnosis, Shadow Ball — one of the best Frontier specials.

05 · Metal Coat

Onix → Steelix

Trade holding Metal Coat. Onix not Hoenn-native — FRLG/Colosseum import only.

06 · Metal Coat

Scyther → Scizor

Trade holding Metal Coat. Scyther in Safari Zone (FRLG); Heracross is the Hoenn analogue.

07 · Dragon Scale

Seadra → Kingdra

Trade holding Dragon Scale. Horsea found via Sapphire/FRLG trades; rare in Emerald natively.

08 · King's Rock

Poliwhirl → Politoed

Trade holding King’s Rock. Alternate Poliwhirl path is Water Stone for Poliwrath.

09 · King's Rock

Slowpoke → Slowking

Trade holding King’s Rock. Alternate Slowpoke path is Lv 37 for Slowbro.

10 · Up-Grade

Porygon → Porygon2

Trade holding Up-Grade. Porygon FRLG-import only (Game Corner prize).

11 · Deep Sea Tooth

Clamperl → Huntail

Trade holding Deep Sea Tooth. Hoenn-native trade evo (Clamperl found via Dive).

12 · Deep Sea Scale

Clamperl → Gorebyss

Trade holding Deep Sea Scale. Same Clamperl, different held item, different path.

The single-cart problem

The easiest single-player trade evolution path is two GBAs and a link cable, even if both carts are yours. Use a wireless or link adapter even with a flash cart on a real GBA — the receiving Pokemon evolves on receipt.

Section VI

Friendship
evolutions

Five friendship evolutions exist for Hoenn-accessible Pokemon. The protocol is the same for all five: catch in a Luxury Ball, equip Soothe Bell, walk and battle until friendship hits 220, then level up.

Hoenn-native

Golbat → Crobat

poison flying

Catch Zubat in Granite Cave, Mt. Pyre, or Meteor Falls. Golbat at Lv 22. Soothe Bell + Luxury Ball cuts friendship time roughly in half. The single best return on a friendship grind in Emerald — Crobat’s Speed and Sludge Bomb / Aerial Ace coverage justify every step.

Protocol

Luxury Ball capture (2× gain) + Soothe Bell (1.5×, stacks) + level up via grinding + walk 256 steps repeatedly. Crobat in <30 minutes of optimized play.

Bred only

Pichu → Pikachu

electric

Pichu only obtainable by breeding Pikachu (hold Light Ball for the egg-move boost). Pikachu itself is rare in Safari Zone. Don’t evolve to Raichu yet — remember Pikachu learns Thunder at Lv 50 and Raichu does NOT (see Section II).

Bred only

Azurill → Marill

normal

Misleadingly NOT a low-friendship evolution despite Azurill’s nervous flavor text. Hatch from a Marill or Azumarill egg. Friendship ≥ 220, then level up. Note Azurill is Normal — it gains the Water type only after evolving into Marill.

Bred only

Igglybuff → Jigglypuff

normal

Not native to Hoenn wild; hatch from a Jigglypuff/Wigglytuff egg if traded in. Cleffa → Clefairy follows the same pattern; both are baby-form-only obtained by breeding their later forms.

The fast-friendship protocol

Luxury Ball (2× gains) + Soothe Bell (1.5×, stacks) + walk constantly (256 steps = +3 with both bonuses) + grind levels (+9 to +18 per level depending on threshold) + use stat-lowering berries on the Pokemon (+10 each). Vitamins help too but expensive at 9,800 each.

The Everstone trick

Held by a Pokemon, Everstone blocks evolution permanently until removed. More importantly: held by the female parent at the Day Care, the offspring inherits her Nature 50% of the time. The primary Gen 3 method for nature-locking your team.

In Gen 3, Magneton is the final form — there’s no Magnezone. Magnezone’s “Lv 30 in a magnetic field” trigger arrived in Gen 4.

No de-evolution

There is NO way to de-evolve a Pokemon in Gen 3 — the form-change items of Gen 6+ don’t exist. Once you press through evolve, the pre-form is locked away unless you breed a fresh egg. B-cancel deliberately; the prompt fires again next level-up.

Section VIII

Feebas to
Milotic

The most baroque evolution path in Hoenn. Feebas spawns on six random tiles out of ~600 on Route 119. Once found, you feed it Dry-flavor Pokeblocks until its hidden contest Beauty stat caps. Then one level-up.

No. 349 · Feebas
→
No. 350 · Milotic
Step 01

Find Feebas

Spawns on 6 random tiles out of ~600 on Route 119. Tile selection is tied to your Trainer ID — static for one save file. Use the Super Rod and fish tile-by-tile until you hook one. Save the location like a treasure map.

RATE — 50% on Super Rod once you’re on a Feebas tile.

Step 02

Max Beauty

Feed Dry-flavored Pokeblocks until Beauty stat caps. Best berries: Pamtre (highest single-flavor Dry yield), Spelon, Nomel, Rabuta. Use a Berry Blender with 3 NPCs (PokeNav match call) for Lv 23+ blocks.

QUALITY — Aim for Lv 23+ blocks. NPC parents in Slateport/Lilycove offer free quick blends.

Step 03

Level once

Once Beauty is maxed, Feebas evolves into Milotic on its next level-up. Even one Rare Candy triggers it. Milotic with Recover + Marvel Scale + Ice Beam is a Frontier-grade wall.

RESULT — Marvel Scale: +50% Def while statused. Self-Toxic for permanent boost.

Why this is the only path

Milotic in vanilla Emerald is reachable only through Feebas + max Beauty. There is no wild Milotic, no trade Milotic, no event Milotic in this cartridge. Wallace’s ace at the 8th gym uses one, but he won’t hand his over. This is the path.

Sheen cap

Sheen caps at 255 — once a Pokemon has eaten 255 sheen worth of blocks, it can never eat another. Plan your blocks BEFORE feeding to avoid hard-bricking the contest stat ceiling. Reset berries (Grepa = +Beauty) double as EV resets AND Beauty boosters.

Section IX

Rare Candy vs
natural training

Rare Candy hands you a level for free but yields zero EVs. The Pokemon’s stats trail equivalent natural-leveled team members until EVs catch up. Knowing when to candy is the difference between a competitive Pokemon and a deficit Pokemon.

Salamence · the candy-vs-EV case study

The cartridge offers two parallel ways to gain levels: Rare Candy (instant, no battle) and the EV-bearing kind (battle-derived). They look identical on the level-up screen, but a Rare-Candied Lv 50 Salamence loses by ~12% damage on every hit to an EV-trained Lv 50 Salamence with the same Nature and IVs. EVs aren’t optional — they’re half the stat curve.

The rule that almost always works: battle and EV-train first, THEN Rare Candy to hit your target level. Vitamins fast-forward the first 100 EVs per stat. Reset berries fix mistakes.

What candy DOES

Adds exactly enough EXP to ding the next level. Ignores EXP-table tier. Cannot be used at Lv 100. Triggers normal evolution checks (level + friendship/Beauty conditions).

What candy DOESN'T

Does NOT grant EVs. Does NOT increase friendship. Does NOT trigger Pokerus or Macho Brace effects (no battle = no EVs).

The 252 / 510 cap

Each Pokemon gets 510 total EVs and 252 max per stat (any 253-255 are wasted). 252 EVs in a stat = +63 to that stat at Lv 100 (halved at Lv 50).

When TO use Rare Candy

Five legitimate cases

  • After EV-training; pushing Lv 49 → 50 for the Battle Frontier auto-cap
  • On a Pokemon you’ve already maxed 510 EVs — pure level gain
  • To break a “won’t obey” wall after a new badge (1–2 candies)
  • On rentals/gift Pokemon you don’t care about EV-optimizing
  • Last few levels (Lv 95–100) when EV gain plateaus at 510 anyway
When NOT to use Rare Candy

Four traps

  • While EV training — every candied level is a level you’re not gaining EVs
  • On a Pokemon you plan to Frontier-rent at Lv 100 (Frontier auto-levels to 50)
  • Before knowing your Nature — low-level stats reveal Nature; candy past it and you’ve burned info
  • On Pokemon you intend to breed (parents pass IVs regardless of level)
Natural vs candied: the math

Two identical Adamant Salamence at Lv 50, one fully EV-trained (252 Atk / 252 Spe / 4 HP) and the other Rare-Candied with no EVs. EV-trained Atk: +15 stat at Lv 50. Candied: +0. Real fight result: ~12% higher damage on every hit, plus 7 more Speed points — outspeeding the candied mirror by exactly enough to flip the priority order.

TL;DR

Battle and EV-train first. THEN Rare Candy to hit your target level. Vitamins are the fast-forward button for the first 100 EVs per stat. Reset berries fix mistakes. Save your Lilycove Lottery candies for a Pokemon you’ve already trained.

Closing transmission

Hold B. Read the level-up.
Decide deliberately.

Every evolution is a one-way door. Most have movepool consequences you’ll feel five badges later. The B button is the only way back — use it.
