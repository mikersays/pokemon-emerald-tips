Chapter 02 · Endgame Gauntlet

The Elite Four
& Champion

Five battles, no Pokemon Center between them. Sidney opens with Dark, Phoebe drops Ghosts, Glacia freezes the floor, Drake brings dragons, and Wallace closes the hall with a wall of Water. Bring six fully healed Pokemon, a stack of Full Restores, and a Revive or three.

No. 01 · Dark Hall S dark

Sidney

Ever Grande · First of the Four

Ace
Absol · Lv 49
Weakness
Fighting, Bug
Watch out
Swagger lines — raises your Atk but confuses
Counters
Hariyama (Brick Break), Blaziken (Sky Uppercut), Heracross (Megahorn)
Item recs
Persim Berry · Full Restores × 4
Team roster
Lv 46 Mightyena Crunch · Roar
Lv 48 Shiftry Fake Out · Extrasensory
Lv 46 Cacturne Needle Arm · Leech Seed
Lv 48 Sharpedo Crunch · Surf
Lv 49 Absol Razor Wind · SD
Strategy. Hariyama with Brick Break or Cross Chop, Blaziken with Sky Uppercut, or Heracross with Megahorn rolls this whole team. Watch for Swagger — it raises your Attack but confuses you. Bring a Persim Berry or use a Special Attacker. Shiftry’s Fake Out forces a flinch turn 1, so don’t lead with your sweeper at full HP commit.

FIELD TIP — Cacturne’s Sand Veil + Leech Seed stalls hard if you can’t one-shot. Bring a Repel for clean accuracy.

No. 02 · Ghost Hall P ghost

Phoebe

Ever Grande · Second of the Four

Ace
Dusclops · Lv 51
Weakness
Dark, Ghost
Watch out
Sableye is Dark/Ghost — no type weakness
Counters
Sharpedo (Crunch), Houndoom (Crunch), Absol (Bite/Crunch)
Item recs
Toxic on Sableye · avoid Psychic types
Team roster
Lv 48 Dusclops Shadow Punch · Curse
Lv 49 Banette Shadow Ball · Wisp
Lv 50 Sableye Faint Attack · Attract
Lv 49 Banette Skill Swap · T-bolt
Lv 51 Dusclops Earthquake · Ice Beam
Strategy. Dark moves are super effective on every Pokemon she runs. Sharpedo’s Crunch, Houndoom’s Crunch, or Absol’s Bite/Crunch all dismantle her. Sableye is the trickiest — Dark/Ghost has no type weakness, so use Earthquake or wear it down with Toxic. Avoid bringing Psychic types: Phoebe’s Ghosts pack Crunch and Faint Attack.

FIELD TIP — Banette’s Will-O-Wisp halves your Atk — use Special Attackers as your primary chip.

No. 03 · Ice Hall G ice

Glacia

Ever Grande · Third of the Four

Ace
Walrein · Lv 53
Weakness
Fire, Fighting, Rock, Steel
Watch out
Walrein’s Sheer Cold — one-hit KO
Counters
Blaziken (Blaze Kick), Hariyama (Brick Break), Manectric vs Walrein
Item recs
Lum Berry on lead · Yellow Flute for Attract
Team roster
Lv 50 Sealeo Body Slam · Encore
Lv 50 Glalie Ice Beam · Crunch
Lv 52 Sealeo Ice Ball · Attract
Lv 52 Glalie Shadow Ball · Hail
Lv 53 Walrein Sheer Cold (!)
Strategy. All her Pokemon are pure Ice except Walrein (Ice/Water). Blaziken with Blaze Kick or Flamethrower obliterates the gym; Hariyama with Brick Break is also fantastic. Watch for Walrein’s Sheer Cold — it’s a one-hit KO, so save before engaging. Walrein is doubly weak to Electric due to Water typing; Manectric or Magneton can revenge-kill it.

FIELD TIP — Sealeo’s Encore can lock you into a Bulk Up turn. Don’t set up against it.

No. 04 · Dragon Hall D dragon

Drake

Ever Grande · Fourth of the Four

Ace
Salamence · Lv 55
Weakness
Ice, Dragon
Watch out
Earthquake on Flygon & Salamence
Counters
Walrein (Ice Beam), Glalie (Ice Beam), Lapras if traded
Item recs
Stack of Ice Beam PP Ups · do NOT lead Magneton
Team roster
Lv 52 Shelgon Dragon Claw · Protect
Lv 54 Altaria DD · Aerial Ace
Lv 53 Flygon Earthquake · Crunch
Lv 53 Flygon Sandstorm · Fly
Lv 55 Salamence Dragon Claw · Crunch
Strategy. Ice Beam is your best friend. Walrein, Glalie, or a traded Lapras work great. Salamence and Altaria are doubly weak to Ice (Dragon/Flying). Beware Earthquake on Flygon and Salamence — don’t lead Magneton. If you’ve faced a Kingdra-line trainer in the Frontier you’ll know: Dragon/Water is only weak to Dragon, so keep a Dragon Claw user in reserve.

FIELD TIP — Shelgon’s Protect wastes turns. Use the gap to set up Calm Mind or chip with status.

No. 05 · Champion W water

Wallace

Sootopolis-born · Hoenn Champion

Final Boss
Format

Six Pokemon. Wallace is the only Champion in the franchise to specialize in Water.

Ace
Milotic · Lv 58
Weakness
Mostly Electric/Grass — but Ludicolo only weak to Bug/Flying/Poison
Watch out
Milotic’s Recover + Marvel Scale
Counters
Manectric, Magneton, Sceptile, Crobat (vs Ludicolo)
Item recs
Thunder Wave on Milotic · Full Restores × 6
Team roster
Lv 57 Wailord Water Spout · Rest
Lv 55 Tentacruel Sludge Bomb · Toxic
Lv 56 Ludicolo Giga Drain · Leech Seed
Lv 56 Whiscash Earthquake · Amnesia
Lv 56 Gyarados Hyper Beam · DD
Lv 58 Milotic Recover · Ice Beam
Strategy. Manectric or Raikou-tier Electric attackers shred most of his team. The catch is Ludicolo — bring Sceptile, Beautifly, or a Crobat to handle it. Whiscash is Water/Ground and 4× weak to Grass. Gyarados is 4× weak to Electric. Milotic with Recover + Marvel Scale is the wall — status it (paralyze with Thunder Wave) before chunking with Electric.

FIELD TIP — Wailord opens with Water Spout at full HP for massive damage. Lead with a sturdy switch-in (Manectric’s Lightning Rod baits nothing here, but Magneton tanks it).
