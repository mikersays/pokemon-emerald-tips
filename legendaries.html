Chapter 03 · Beyond the Eight Badges

The Hoenn
legendaries

Ten captures, ten puzzles, ten quiet endings to a long playthrough. The Weather Trio anchors Hoenn mythology; the Eon Twins roam its skies; the Regis sleep behind Braille and a Wailord. Below: where to find each, what gates them, and the math of the perfect ball.

Section I

The Weather Trio

Groudon raises continents, Kyogre floods them, Rayquaza ends the argument. All three sit at Lv 70.

No. 383 · Lv 70 G ground

Groudon

Terra Cave · Roaming Anchor

Location
Terra Cave (rotates — check Hoenn TV news)
Ability
Drought — boosts Fire, weakens Water
Moves
Earthquake · Fire Blast · Hyper Beam · Bulk Up
Best ball
Timer Ball after turn 10 · Dusk Ball at night
Access requirements
  • Beat the Elite Four.
  • Speak to Professor Cozmo and check the news on TV in any Pokemon Center.
  • Terra Cave’s location rotates — check the Hoenn news report each day.
Catching tips. False Swipe with Sceptile to leave 1 HP, then status it (Hypnosis from Gardevoir or Thunder Wave). Drought boosts its Fire moves and weakens Water, so don’t try to chip with Surf. Use Ultra Balls or a Timer Ball after 10+ turns. Strong, bulky, and packing Bulk Up — don’t let it set up.

FIELD TIP — Soft-reset before each capture attempt to roll a different Nature.

No. 382 · Lv 70 K water

Kyogre

Marine Cave · Roaming Anchor

Location
Marine Cave (random water route after Sootopolis)
Ability
Drizzle — doubles Water damage, weakens Fire
Watch out
Sheer Cold — OHKO move
Best ball
Timer Ball · Net Ball (Water type)
Access requirements
  • Beat the Elite Four.
  • Marine Cave appears on a random water route — check Hoenn TV news.
  • Surf required to reach it.
Catching tips. Drizzle forces rain, doubling its own Water move damage and weakening Fire. Don’t bring a Fire attacker. Use a Whismur/Loudred with Sing for sleep, or a Gengar with Hypnosis. Save before engaging — Kyogre’s moveset includes Sheer Cold, and a one-shot KO will end the encounter. Same Timer Ball math as Groudon.

FIELD TIP — Net Ball gets a 3× modifier on Water types — arguably better than Timer Ball before turn 10.

No. 384 · Lv 70 R dragon

Rayquaza

Sky Pillar · Route 131

680 BST
Typing
dragon flying
Moves
Outrage · Extreme Speed · Fly · Rest
Watch out
Outrage hits hard — can KO False Swipers
Best ball
Timer Ball after ~30 turns · Ultra Ball
Access requirements
  • Reach Sky Pillar via Mach Bike navigation — the cracked floors require speed.
  • Climb 5 floors of bike puzzles.
  • Story-mandatory encounter to settle Groudon vs Kyogre. Knocked out? Re-encounter post-Hall of Fame.
Catching tips. Highest base stat total of any Pokemon you can catch on the cartridge. Don’t let Outrage lock it in if you’re using a Dragon — the recoil-confusion is brutal. False Swipe doesn’t work (Outrage hits back hard). Lower with strong neutral moves, then status (paralyze ideally — it can’t sleep with Rest unless you remove Chesto Berry first). Save before engaging.

FIELD TIP — Knock off the Chesto Berry with a Trick or Knock Off user before initiating sleep.

Section II

The Eon Twins

Latios and Latias roam Hoenn after the 7th badge. One via the news event, one via the Eon Ticket.

No. 381 · Lv 40 L dragon

Latios

Roaming Hoenn · Post-7th Badge

Typing
dragon psychic
Behavior
Roaming — flees on turn 1
Tools needed
Mean Look (Crobat) or Block
Best ball
Master Ball recommended · Net Ball after Mean Look
Access requirements
  • Watch the news at home with your mom after the 7th badge.
  • Choice in dialogue determines which Lati you get; the other comes via Eon Ticket.
  • Eon Ticket (Mystery Gift event item) lets you sail to Southern Island for the other Lati.
Catching tips. A roaming legendary — it flees turn 1. Use Mean Look with a Crobat (Lv 30+ learns it via breeding/move tutor) or Block. Repels gate lower-level encounters so you only run into Latios while biking. Use the Master Ball if you want to skip the chase entirely — this is the most justified Master Ball use in the game.

FIELD TIP — Track its location on the PokeNav map. It moves between routes after each encounter.

No. 380 · Lv 40 L psychic

Latias

Roaming Hoenn · Post-7th Badge

Typing
dragon psychic
Behavior
Roaming — same as Latios
Profile
Slightly more defensive than Latios
Best ball
Master Ball · Net Ball after Mean Look
Access requirements
  • Identical event chain to Latios — news choice determines roamer.
  • Eon Ticket unlocks the other Lati on Southern Island.
Catching tips. Identical mechanics to Latios. Slightly more defensively oriented. Both have access to a Mega Stone in later games, but in Emerald you’ll just want them for the Soul Dew — a held item that boosts their Sp. Atk and Sp. Def by 50%.

FIELD TIP — Soul Dew is held by the Southern Island encounter. Don’t Knock Off your own Lati.

Section III

The Regi Trio

All three sit behind the Sealed Chamber Braille puzzle. You’ll need a Wailord at the head of your party and a Relicanth at the tail to read the script — only then do their caves open. All three are Lv 40, all three carry Explosion. Bring Damp.

No. 377 · Lv 40 R rock

Regirock

Desert Ruins · Route 111

Defenses
200 base Defense
Moves
Curse · Superpower · Explosion
Counter abilities
Damp (Poliwhirl, Wooper) blocks Explosion
Best ball
Ultra Ball · Timer Ball
Access requirements
  • Wailord head + Relicanth tail of your party.
  • Read the Braille in Sealed Chamber (under the Route 134 dive spot).
  • Inside Desert Ruins: 2 steps down from center, 2 right, then Rock Smash.
Catching tips. Don’t let it Explode — it WILL kill itself. Bring Pokemon with the Damp ability (Poliwhirl/Wooper) to block Explosion entirely. Use Sleep status; chip with Special Attackers since its Defense is 200 base.

FIELD TIP — Special Attackers like Gardevoir (Calm Mind + Psychic) bypass its physical wall.

No. 378 · Lv 40 R ice

Regice

Island Cave · Route 105

Defenses
200 base Sp. Def
Moves
Includes Explosion — bring Damp
Approach
Use physical attackers — Special is pitiful
Best ball
Timer Ball after 10+ turns
Access requirements
  • Same Relicanth/Wailord party requirement.
  • Inside the cave, stand in the center for 2 minutes without moving.
  • (Older sources say walk a counter-clockwise lap; the proven method is just stand still.)
Catching tips. Massive Special Defense (200 base). Use physical attackers to chip it down — Hariyama with Brick Break, or a high-Atk Steel-type. Has Explosion, so a Damp partner is mandatory. Avoid Special moves; you’ll do pitiful damage even with super effective.

FIELD TIP — Set a real-world timer. The 2-minute window is exact — an early step resets it.

No. 379 · Lv 40 R steel

Registeel

Ancient Tomb · Route 120

Defenses
200 / 200 (balanced)
Moves
Curse · Explosion · Iron Defense
Resistances
Steel resists everything except Fire/Fighting/Ground
Best ball
Ultra Ball · Timer Ball
Access requirements
  • Same Relicanth/Wailord party requirement.
  • Use Fly to access Route 120, walk to the small tomb.
  • Inside, use Flash in the center of the room.
Catching tips. Balanced 200/200 defenses. Status it (paralyze, then put to sleep) and chip with Fighting or Fire moves. Watch for Curse (slows it but raises Atk/Def) and Explosion. Steel typing resists everything except Fire, Fighting, and Ground.

FIELD TIP — A Lv 50+ Hariyama with Brick Break is the cleanest single-Pokemon answer.

Section IV

Event-only Pokemon

These two require Mystery Gift items distributed at long-discontinued Nintendo events. Without trade or hacks, they remain catalog entries.

No. 151 · Lv 30 M psychic

Mew

Faraway Island · Old Sea Map

Event 2006
Required item
Old Sea Map (Mystery Gift)
Encounter
Stealth-track through tall grass on Faraway Island
Tool needed
False Swipe + status
Best ball
Net Ball · Ultra Ball (no Quick Ball in Gen 3)
Catching tips. Mew runs through tall grass on Faraway Island and you have to track it without being spotted (Solid Snake style). Once cornered, it teleports. After enough loops, you battle it at Lv 30. Use False Swipe and a Net or Ultra Ball.

FIELD TIP — Old Sea Map is not legitimately obtainable today without trade or hacking.

No. 386 · Lv 30 D psychic

Deoxys

Birth Island · Aurora Ticket

Speed Forme
Required item
Aurora Ticket (Mystery Gift)
Forme in Emerald
Speed Forme — carries Extreme Speed
Encounter
Triangle puzzle on the island; one shot
Best ball
Ultra Ball · Timer Ball
Catching tips. In Emerald, Deoxys appears in its Speed Forme by default (different from FireRed/LeafGreen, which give Attack/Defense formes). It carries Extreme Speed. Save before the puzzle — solving it spawns Deoxys and you only get one shot. Use False Swipe and Ultra Balls.

FIELD TIP — Speed Forme out-speeds nearly everything. False Swipe with a Pokemon holding a Quick Claw helps.

Editor’s Note

Both event Pokemon are part of the Hoenn dex but cannot be obtained through normal play in 2025. They’re documented here for completeness. If you have a save with a legitimate event Pokemon, treasure it.
